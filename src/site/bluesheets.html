The Errors

  1. Past Tense- Use the present tense in writing about a literary work
  2. Sentences- Write complete, correct sentences. Avoid the following:
    1. Fragment
    2. Run-On
    3. Comma Splice
  3. 1st or 2nd Person- "I", "me", "my", "we", "us", "our", "you", "your"
  4. Vague This or Which- Do not use this or which to refer to a clause
  5. Number Disagreement- A subject and verb must agree in number, and a pronoun must agree in number with it's antecedent
  6. Pronoun Case- Put pronouns in the appropriate case: subjunctive, objective, possessive
  7. Ambiguous Pronoun- Avoid using a pronoun in situations where it would be difficult to decipher the subject of it
  8. Apostrophe Error- Use an apostrophe to indicate possession, not to indicate that a noun is plural
    Due to the contextual nature of this error, we can only focus your attention upon situations where this error would occur.
  9. Passive Voice- Write in the active voice
  10. Dangling Modifier- Avoid modifiers with which the consequential phrase is not the subject of the modifying phrase
    Due to the contextual nature of this error, we can only focus your attention upon situations where this error would occur.
  11. Faulty Parallelism- Use identical grammatical forms to coordinate parallel ideas
  12. Progressive tense- Avoid using the progressive tense
  13. Incorrect Possesiveness with Gerunds- Recognize gerunds and use possessives accordingly
  14. Quotation Error- Quote accurately, sparingly and be aware of punctuation in a quotation
